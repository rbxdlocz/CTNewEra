I
The Chronicle of CT

Every era CT has lived through was earned. Nothing was handed to it. This is the record of what was built, what was fought, and what was won — before the Internal War began.

I
Founding Era
Built From Nothing
CT was founded to solve a real problem — cross-teaming had infected NBTF and nobody was doing anything about it. A small group decided that wasn't acceptable. CT was born reactionary, small, and scrappy. It had no endorsement, no established reputation, and no guarantee of survival. What it had was a mission worth fighting for and the will to fight for it. That was enough.
~70 founding members
II
The Defining Moment
CT Fights Alone — CT Wins
Early in CT's existence, a major conflict erupted. An external faction redirected its aggression toward CT after CT intervened unprompted to defend another group. The group CT defended stepped back. CT stood alone and fought anyway. The outcome was a CT victory — and it created something no policy or document could manufacture: internal pride, external respect, and a reputation built through action rather than words. That moment wrote CT's identity before the faction was old enough to name it.
III
Growth Era
From Scrappy to Institutional
CT grew. What started as a reactive enforcement unit evolved into something more structured — executive roles, company hierarchies, training pipelines, alliance frameworks, a democratic succession plan. The organization built itself around a mission, and that gave it staying power. Over time CT became one of the more recognizable factions in NBTF — not through self-declaration, but through presence. People knew the name. Whether that reputation was earned or just loudly accumulated is for others to judge. Either way, CT kept showing up. Each confirmed report meant deploying Ravens and Enforcers across both teams, racing to reset the core before it exploded while holding off Facility and Rebel MBRs at the same time. 119 operations won. 23 lost — usually when the damage was too far gone or the pressure too heavy to hold.
Peaked at 250+ members
119W · 23L — Core Recovery Record
IV
The Eternal War
CT's Primary Mission — Declared and Waged
No declaration was ever needed. CT's position on cross-teaming was understood from the beginning — if you cross-teamed in a server where CT had presence, you'd be found. Ravens gathered intel from the Facility side. Enforcers pressed from the Rebel side. When a report was confirmed, CT split across both teams and moved on the core — repairing damage under time pressure while holding off whoever was in the way. It wasn't a formal campaign. It was an operating standard, run consistently, every session. Cross-teamers learned what CT was by running into it.
Eternal War — Active
V
External Threats
The Inner Alliance
The Internal War did not always define CT's two companies as rivals. When external forces — other factions, coordinated hostilities, threats to CT's members or its standing in NBTF — arose, Ravens and Enforcers unified completely. The External Threat Clause was born from real experience. CT learned that its greatest strength was not one company's dominance over the other — it was both companies moving as one when something outside demanded it. That unity was never broken by an outside force.
VI
Victory
The Eternal War — Declared Won
NBTF officially adopted anti-cross-teaming enforcement as part of the game's own policy. The problem CT was built to solve was formally acknowledged and addressed by the game itself. The Eternal War is over. CT operated so visibly, so persistently, and so effectively that the game had no choice but to act. The mission is complete. The war is won. CT's founding purpose has been fulfilled — not by compromise or drift, but by total strategic victory.
Eternal War — Won
Mission Complete
The Legacy

The crossteaming report command — the heartbeat of CT's original mission — is preserved as a permanent legacy system. The historic record of the Eternal War remains viewable. CT's founding mission does not disappear. It becomes the bedrock of everything built after it.

II
The Eternal War Is Over

CT was built to eradicate cross-teaming in NBTF. We did it so effectively, so visibly, and so persistently that the game itself was forced to adopt our mission as official policy. The Eternal War ended not in defeat — but in total victory.

Most factions dissolve because they fail. CT is still standing because it won. With no external enemy left to fight, CT's two companies — Ravens and Enforcers — faced a choice: dissolve, drift, or turn to face each other.

They chose to face each other.

What This Is

The Internal War is not CT falling apart. It is organized, honorable, and intentional conflict — two armies bound by the same history and the same Eternal War they won together, now locked in competition for dominance. The War Council was formed to keep it honorable. The Internal War has begun.

The faction voted. The executives deliberated. The decision was made democratically, transparently, and with full participation. CT enters its next era not by decree — but by choice.

V
Public Systems

The Internal War introduces public-facing mechanics that connect the broader NBTF community to CT's conflict — making CT visible, relevant, and worth engaging with whether you're a member or not.

System 01
For-Hire
Public Reinforcement

Any NBTF player can request CT backup through CTBOT. Need numbers for a fight, backup during a skirmish, or bodies for a push? Submit the request — available Ravens or Enforcers respond based on which side fits your needs. Mission complete earns the responding CT member CTC. Low commitment, real impact. You don't have to be in CT to call on CT.

System 02
Hitman
Contract Kills

Any NBTF player — including CT members — can place a hit on a target through CTBOT. Submit the target's name and a CTC bounty. A Raven or Enforcer takes the contract, hunts the target, and submits proof. Bounty paid on confirmed kill. Payout scales with the target's rank — the higher the target, the bigger the reward.

One active hit per target at a time
48-hour cooldown before another hit can be placed on the same person after completion
System 03
War Bank
Central CTC Treasury

One central CTC pool governed neutrally by the War Council. Both companies draw from it for for-hire payouts, hitman contracts, and event rewards. Neither company can access it unilaterally. Winning official war events earns the victorious company a larger share of that cycle's CTC distribution — merit-based stakes, neutrally managed.

System 04
War Standings
Season Score Tracking

A live running score tracking both companies across all official Internal War engagements — battles, sanctioned RP conflicts, and war events. Updated publicly after every official result. Seasons reset on a scheduled cycle with all records preserved. Simple, visible, and honest: the standings tell you exactly which company is winning right now.

The External Threat Clause
When The Internal War Pauses

When the War Council unanimously determines that an external force poses a significant threat to CT, its members, or NBTF stability — the Internal War is immediately suspended. Both companies fall under unified command for the duration of the external conflict.

Unanimous War Council vote required to declare an external threat
Both Czars can contest the declaration but cannot veto it alone
War standings frozen during suspension — resume exactly where they left off
Unanimous vote required to lift suspension and resume the Internal War

Ravens and Enforcers are rivals. But they are CT rivals — bound by the same history and the same Eternal War they won together. When something outside threatens what they built, the old alliance surfaces without hesitation. Not because they like each other. Because no outsider gets to touch what's theirs.

VII
The Event Structure

The Internal War runs on events. Existing CT events will be retrofitted to fit the new identity — reframed around the Raven vs Enforcer conflict. New events specific to the Internal War will be developed as the era matures.

OW
Official War Events
The centerpiece of the Internal War. Scheduled and announced server-wide. Full Raven vs Enforcer battle with kills, objectives, and core outcomes tracked. Pre-battle declarations, post-battle reports in character. Winner takes the round — standings updated publicly. These are CT's biggest moments and the primary driver of the season score.
PB
Practice Battles
Raven vs Enforcer — smaller, unranked skirmishes between official events. No standings impact. Tactics developed, new members get real experience, the rivalry stays active between scheduled events. Any Field Pacesetter or above can call a practice battle at their discretion.
PS
Public Server Events
CT takes the Internal War live — directly to the NBTF server population. Both companies deploy on public servers and wage their conflict in front of the entire game's community. Open for anyone watching to see CT in action, understand the conflict, and find out how to get involved. The Internal War is not just a CT story. It plays out where everyone can witness it.
IS
Intra-Squad Battles
Same-company competition — Ravens vs Ravens, Enforcers vs Enforcers. Determines internal standings and identifies top performers for official war events. Builds squad chemistry within each company. No cross-company stakes but significant internal implications for who gets deployed when it counts.
DE
Defection Events
Public events open to the whole server as spectators. The Hunt, The Gauntlet, The Sponsored Crossing — CT's most dramatic recurring content. High-rank defection attempts draw the entire server's attention. Win or lose, every defection event feeds the lore, the legend, and the rivalry.
TN
Tournaments
Structured competitive events — 1v1s, squad brackets, cross-company matchups. Season-based with standings implications. Give individual members a path to personal recognition beyond company affiliation. The best fighters from both sides compete for individual legacy.
Redevelopment Note

All existing CT events will be reviewed as the Internal War era launches. Those that fit get retrofitted with new framing and updated scoring. Those that don't get archived respectfully alongside the Eternal War records. New events specific to this era will be developed collaboratively by both companies.

VIII
RP, Lore & Storytelling

The Internal War comes with a living narrative — and that narrative is one of CT's most valuable new assets. RP and lore are not decoration. They are a necessary component that keeps the Internal War coherent, immersive, and worth following beyond individual events.

CT is building this as it goes. The foundation is strong — the history, the companies, the rivalry, the conflict. The storytelling layer develops with it.

01
A New Audience

CT never had a dedicated space for players drawn to faction lore and character-driven gameplay. The Internal War creates one. Players who want immersion alongside PvP now have a reason to join and a narrative to engage with that goes beyond any single match.

02
Seasonal Arcs

Each season of the Internal War has a narrative arc. Standings are not just numbers — they are the story of which company dominated that chapter of CT's history. Season endings get documented. Upsets get remembered. The faction's history writes itself through competition.

03
War Declarations

Every official war event opens with a formal declaration — written in character, posted publicly before the battle begins. Each company makes their statement. After the event, the result is announced with the same weight. The Internal War always feels like it matters because it always sounds like it matters.

04
Retention Through Story

When the server is quiet, the narrative keeps people connected. When someone returns after inactivity, the story gives them something to catch up on and a reason to re-engage. The Internal War's lore is CT's longest running event — it never ends.

IX
The Recruitment Trial

Joining CT in the Internal War era is not just signing up. Before a recruit chooses their company, they go through three mandatory experiences — one with each company, then one deciding battle. The choice they make is informed, not a guess.

I
The Raven Mission
The recruit accompanies Raven Company on an actual operation. No obligation, no pressure. They see how Ravens move, think, communicate, and fight — intelligence-focused, precision-oriented, facility-sided. One real mission. Not a demo. Not a tour.
II
The Enforcer Mission
The recruit accompanies Enforcer Company on an actual operation. Same format — real mission, real combat, real team dynamic. Brute force, aggression, rebel-sided. The recruit experiences the full contrast between both companies firsthand before making any decision.
III
The Deciding Battle
The recruit chooses a side for one live Internal War battle — no permanent consequences for this choice. Trial run. They fight alongside their chosen company in a real engagement. At the end they make their final allegiance declaration. Whatever they choose becomes their company — until they use their one lifetime defection.
Why This Works

Recruits make an informed decision based on real experience — not aesthetics or a coin flip. It also puts new members into active events before they've officially joined a company. Their first three CT experiences are actual combat. That is a better introduction than any document.
